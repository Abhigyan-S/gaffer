FAQ

Common questions.

Everything you need to know before you start.

Is Gaffer really free? ›
Yes, completely free. No subscription, no hidden costs, no premium tier. Gaffer is open-source and hosted on GitHub Pages. You can even download the file and run it offline.
Do I need to create an account? ›
No account needed. Open the app and start using it immediately. Your squad and session data is stored in your browser's local storage, so it persists between visits without needing a login.
Does it work on iPad and tablets? ›
Yes. Gaffer is fully touch-optimised. Drag players with your finger, tap to switch modes, and use it in the dressing room on any iPad or Android tablet. It also works perfectly on laptops and desktops.
How does the AI work? ›
Gaffer uses Claude (by Anthropic) for all AI features. When you ask for a formation analysis, team talk, scout report, or development plan, it sends your inputs to Claude and displays the response. The AI understands football tactics at a high level and is tuned for grassroots coaching contexts.
Can I use it offline? ›
The tactics board, squad manager, session planner, and match log all work offline. The AI features (formation analysis, team talks, scout reports, etc.) require an internet connection since they call the AI API.
How do I draw pass arrows and run lines? ›
Switch to "Pass arrow" or "Run line" mode using the buttons at the bottom of the tactics board. Then tap and drag from any point on the pitch to draw the line. Yellow solid arrows are passes, blue dashed lines are player runs. Switch back to "Select" mode to drag players again.
How do I edit a player's shirt number or name? ›
In Select mode, double-tap (or double-click) any player circle to edit their number or name. On tablet you can also long-press to get a context menu with options to edit, switch team colour, or remove the player.
What are phases and how do I use them? ›
Phases let you save multiple board states as steps in a play. Set up your first tactical shape, tap "Save phase" and name it (e.g. "Press trigger"). Then move players into the next position, save again as "After press", and so on. During team meetings, tap through the phases to walk your players through the full sequence.
Does the scouting tool need stats data? ›
No. That's the point. You just type what you observe — their formation, playing style, danger players, strengths and weaknesses — and the AI turns it into a full tactical breakdown and game plan. No Opta data, no subscriptions, no access to professional analytics required.
Can I save my tactics as an image to share with players? ›
Yes. Hit "Save image" in the tactics toolbar and it downloads the current pitch as a PNG. You can then WhatsApp it to your players, print it, or use it in a presentation.
Is the code open source? ›
Yes. The full source is on GitHub. You can fork it, modify it, run it locally, or contribute to it. Pull requests are welcome.
